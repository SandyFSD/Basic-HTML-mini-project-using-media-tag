Top 10 Murugar Temple in Tamilnadu

1. Thiruparangundram:

The Arulmigu Subramanya Swami Temple, Tiruchendur is an ancient Hindu temple dedicated to Lord Murugan. It is second among six abodes of Murugan situated in Tamil Nadu, India. It is located in the eastern end of the town Tiruchendur in the district of Thoothukudi, Tamil Nadu, India.


2. Thiruchendur:

The Thiruchendur Murugan Temple attracts millions of devotees every year who come to pay respect to the in-house deity of Lord Murugan. The temple's history is rooted in the story of Lord Murugan's ultimate battle, which is widely believed by many as the purpose of his birth.


3. Palani

Palani is one of the Six Abodes of Murugan, celebrated in Tamil literature and hymns such as Thirupugazh by the 15th-century poet Arunagirinathar. The temple not only embodies Murugan's divine energy but also serves as a place of solace for millions of devotees seeking spiritual guidance.


4. Swamimalai:

Swamimalai is a panchayat town that is a suburb near Kumbakonam in Thanjavur District in the Indian state of Tamil Nadu. It lies on the banks of river Kaveri and is the site of the fourth of the six abodes of the Hindu deity Murugan.


5. Thiruttani:

Tiruttani is primarily famous for the Arulmigu Subramanya Swamy Temple, a revered Hindu temple dedicated to Lord Muruga (also known as Lord Subramanya). It is one of the six abodes of Lord Muruga (Arupadaiveedu) in Tamil Nadu. The town is also known as the birthplace of Dr. Sarvepalli Radhakrishnan, the first President of India.


6. Pazhamudhircholai:

Pazhamudircholai is the sixth (and final) of the six holy shrines of Lord Murugan, known as the Arupadai Veedu. It is located in the hills near Madurai, Tamil Nadu, and is also referred to as the Solaimalai Murugan Temple. The temple is renowned for its association with the Tamil poet Avvaiyar and a legend where Lord Murugan tested her knowledge


7. Vadapalani Murugan Temple

Only in this temple, one can see Murugan wearing footwear, a symbol that denotes shedding of arrogance. He is seen in a standing pose with his left foot forward to indicate he is in a rush to help his devotees. Devotees offer silver and gold as offerings.


8. Marudamalai Murugan Temple

The Marudhamalai Murugan Temple, or the Maruthamalai Marudhachalamurthy Temple, is a 12th-century hill temple situated in Coimbatore, Tamil Nadu, India. Built by Tamil kings during the Sangam period as indicated in the Purananuru, the temple is dedicated to Murugan, a form of Kartikeya.


9. Muthumalai Murugan Temple

Etthapur Muthumalai Murugan Temple is a Murugan temple located in the Putragoundanpalayam suburb of Etthapur in Salem district of the state of Tamil Nadu . [ 1 ] The tallest Murugan statue in the world is located here. The statue is 111 feet tall. The pedestal is 35 feet high. This makes it 146 feet tall.


10. Chennimalai Murugar Temple

Chennimalai is primarily known for the Arulmigu Chennimalai Murugan Temple, a highly revered Hindu shrine dedicated to Lord Murugan, located atop a hill. The town itself is also known as the "Handloom Town" for its significant textile industry, particularly handloom weaving.
